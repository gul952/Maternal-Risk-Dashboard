Provincial Maternal‑Health Risk Analysis for Pakistan

Introduction

Maternal mortality and morbidity remain major challenges in Pakistan. This report examines provincial disparities in antenatal care (ANC) and skilled birth attendance (SBA) using PDHS 2017–18 data, and constructs a composite Risk Score to highlight areas requiring targeted intervention.

Data & Methods

  1. Loaded prov_pdhs_maternal_health.csv and cleaned province names.
  2. Merged with GADM v4.1 Admin‑1 shapefile.
  3. Computed Risk Score via: Risk Score = 100 – (ANC coverage + SBA coverage) / 2.
  4. Generated static bar charts and a static choropleth map.

Conclusion & Key Findings

Key Findings: Among Pakistan’s provinces, Balochistan has the highest maternal-health risk (Risk Score = 67.6), followed by Khyber Pakhtunkhwa. The lowest risk is in Punjab (Risk Score = 33.7).
